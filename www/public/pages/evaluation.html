Evaluation
A hard drive stores ____.


data
A traditional hard drive stores data on ____.


spinning platters
A solid-state drive stores data on ____.


flash memory
A traditional hard drive reads and writes data using a ____.


magnetic head
Hard drives are divided into sections called ____.


sectors
Data loss can happen due to ____ failure.


mechanical
A solid-state drive has no ____ parts.


moving
A traditional hard drive's lifespan is typically around ____ years.


3-5
A solid-state drive's lifespan is measured by the number of ____ cycles.


write
The longer a solid-state drive is unpowered, the more likely it is to ____ data.


lose
Data in a traditional hard drive can last ____ if left untouched.


decades
Cold storage means storing a drive without ____.


power
An SSD loses data faster in cold storage due to its reliance on ____ storage.


flash
If a traditional hard drive is dropped, it may suffer ____ damage.


mechanical
To prevent data loss, it is recommended to create ____ backups.


regular
The process of writing data on a hard drive is called ____.


recording
Hard drives store data in the form of tiny ____.


magnetic fields
A drive that is powered on regularly tends to have a longer ____ than one left idle.


lifespan
SSDs store data in memory cells organized in ____.


blocks
Traditional hard drives use a component called an ____ to spin the platters.


actuator
Data on a drive can be lost permanently if the ____ is severely damaged.


platters
Hard drive data recovery is possible but can be ____.


expensive
A full drive may suffer from performance issues due to ____.


fragmentation
SSDs are preferred for speed, while traditional hard drives are preferred for ____.


capacity
To extend a hard drive's lifespan, avoid ____ temperature changes.


extreme
